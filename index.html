My story

Curiosity first. Then cancer made it personal.

For as long as I can remember, I was drawn to how living things work — dissecting bugs in the backyard, watching surgical procedures online, quietly fascinated by what others found uncomfortable. Science was never an obligation. It was just how I saw the world.

When my mother was diagnosed with cancer, that curiosity found its direction. Watching our family navigate the uncertainty of her illness — and beginning to research the disease myself — made clear what I wanted to spend my life doing. Cancer biology wasn't a field I chose. It chose me.

I finished my bachelor's in three years while working multiple jobs, then moved into a master's program at SFSU conducting cancer research at UCSF. Now I'm interviewing with Oxford's DPhil program — my dream next step — and building toward a career as a physician-scientist.
